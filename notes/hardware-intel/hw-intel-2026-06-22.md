## 1. New Hardware Found

Devices that are now shipping or have launched since the last report, meeting elect-rix deployment criteria.

### 🆕 NVIDIA Jetson T4000
- **Specs:** 1,536-core Blackwell GPU (5th-gen Tensor Cores), 64 GB LPDDR5x (273 GB/s), 12-core Arm Neoverse-V3AE, 1,200 FP4 Sparse TFLOPS
- **Power:** 40W–70W
- **Price:** Module pricing TBD; dev kits expected via partners (ADLINK announced platforms March 2026). Likely $1,000–$1,500 range based on T5000 positioning.
- **Efficiency:** Massive leap over AGX Orin — up to 2× performance at lower power. Pin-compatible with T5000 for carrier board reuse.
- **Availability:** Announced Jan 2026; partner platforms shipping (ADLINK). JetPack 7.1 SDK available.
- **7B+ Performance:** **Exceptional.** NVIDIA benchmarks: Qwen3-30B-A3B at **218 tok/s**, Qwen 3 32B at **68 tok/s**, Mistral 3 14B at **100 tok/s**, Nemotron 12B at **40 tok/s**. These are TensorRT Edge-LLM results with FP4/INT4 quantization.
- **Source:** [NVIDIA Developer Blog (Jan 2026)](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/accelerate-ai-inference-for-edge-and-robotics-with-nvidia-jetson-t4000-and-nvidia-jetpack-7-1/), [ADLINK news](https://www.adlinktech.com/en/news/adlink-edge-ai-platforms-nvidia-thor)
- **Elect-rix impact:** ⭐ **Top-tier edge inference target.** CUDA ecosystem, 64 GB unified memory, and verified 30B+ model throughput make this the most capable edge device we've tracked. Promotes from watch list to primary SMB edge recommendation if dev kit pricing lands under $1,500.

### 🆕 Apple M5 / M5 Pro / M5 Max
- **Specs:**
  - M5: 10-core CPU, 10-core GPU with Neural Accelerators, 16-core NE, 153 GB/s, up to 32 GB
  - M5 Pro: 18-core CPU, up to 20-core GPU, 307 GB/s, up to 64 GB
  - M5 Max: 18-core CPU, up to 40-core GPU, **614 GB/s**, up to 128 GB
- **Power:** ~15–40W sustained (fanless Mini, active laptops)
- **Price:** MacBook Pro M5 from ~$1,599; M5 Pro 48GB ~$2,800–$3,000; M5 Max 128GB ~$3,500–$4,200
- **Efficiency:** **First dedicated matrix hardware in Apple GPU** — Neural Accelerators in every GPU core (~70 TFLOPS FP16 on M5 Max, 4× over M4 Max). Token generation still bandwidth-bound: 614 GB/s yields ~12% improvement over M4 Max for generation, but **3.5–4× TTFT speedup** (prefill is compute-bound, benefits from matrix units).
- **Availability:** M5 shipping since Oct 2025; M5 Pro/Max shipping since Mar 2026
- **7B+ Performance (MLX, 4-bit):**
  - M5 Pro 48GB: Qwen 2.5 32B at **42–50 tok/s** (8K context), 14B at 58–65 tok/s
  - M5 Max 128GB: Llama 3.3 70B at **~30–35 tok/s** (est.), Qwen3-30B MoE 1.25× gen speedup + 3.52× TTFT
  - M5 base 32GB: 7B models 60–80+ tok/s (extrapolated from M4 scaling)
- **Source:** [Apple Newsroom Oct 2025](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2025/10/apple-unleashes-m5-the-next-big-leap-in-ai-performance-for-apple-silicon/), [Apple Newsroom Mar 2026](https://www.apple.com/newsroom/2026/03/apple-debuts-m5-pro-and-m5-max-to-supercharge-the-most-demanding-pro-workflows/), [Contra Collective benchmarks](https://contracollective.com/blog/m4-m5-pro-local-ai-inference-mlx-2026), [Skorppio M5 Max analysis](https://skorppio.com/blog/apple-m5-max-vs-nvidia-ai-deep-dive)
- **Elect-rix impact:** ⭐ **M5 Pro 48GB is the new SMB sweet spot.** 32B model at 42–50 tok/s beats cloud API latency under load. M5 Max 128GB enables 70B locally — relevant for elect-rix "pro" tier. Neural Accelerator + MLX is the easiest deployment path.

### 🆕 AMD Ryzen AI Halo
- **Specs:** Ryzen AI Max+ 395, 128 GB LPDDR5x unified, 60 FP16 TFLOPS GPU, 50 TOPS NPU (XDNA 2), full ROCm Day-0 support on Windows **and** Linux
- **Power:** 120W PSU (small form factor mini-PC)
- **Price:** **$3,999** (vs NVIDIA DGX Spark at $4,699)
- **Efficiency:** AMD claims +7–14% tok/s over DGX Spark on large MoE models (Qwen 3.5-122B, GPT-OSS 120B). Supports up to 200B parameter models locally. ROCm full support on Windows is a differentiator.
- **Availability:** Pre-orders live at Micro Center (US only); shipping imminent
- **7B+ Performance:** Not independently benchmarked yet; AMD claims competitive with DGX Spark on 30B–120B models
- **Source:** [AMD product page](https://www.amd.com/en/products/processors/desktops/ryzen/ryzen-ai-halo.html), [TechPowerUp](https://www.techpowerup.com/349943/pre-orders-for-usd-4000-amd-ryzen-ai-halo-mini-pc-dev-kits-go-live)
- **Elect-rix impact:** Above strict SMB tier ($2,000) but notable as the first x86 unified-memory mini-PC with full ROCm. Watch for price drops and independent benchmarks. If it drops below $3,000, it becomes a strong alternative to Mac Studio for teams that need x86/ROCm.

### 🆕 Intel Arc Pro B70 (Battlemage Pro)
- **Specs:** Xe2-HPG (Battlemage), 32 GB GDDR6 ECC, 367 TOPS INT8, 608 GB/s bandwidth, 230W TBP
- **Power:** 230W (not consumer-tier efficient, but reasonable for workstation)
- **Price:** **$949** per card — less than half the RTX 5090 ($1,999, also 32 GB)
- **Efficiency:** 3× slower bandwidth than RTX 5090 (608 vs 1,792 GB/s), but unmatched VRAM/$ ratio. 4-card config = 128 GB for ~$3,800.
- **Availability:** Shipping now
- **7B+ Performance (vLLM XPU, FP16):**
  - Single card: Llama 3.1 8B at **35.4 tok/s**, DeepSeek R1 8B at **66.9 tok/s**, Qwen2.5 3B at 72.9 tok/s
  - 4-card (TP=4): Llama 3.1 8B at 70.3 tok/s (2× speedup), Qwen3.6-35B MoE at **16.3 tok/s** (conc=1), 122 tok/s (conc=8)
  - Qwen3.6-27B dense at 13.1 tok/s (conc=1), 96 tok/s (conc=8)
- **Source:** [Puget Systems multi-GPU review](https://www.pugetsystems.com/labs/articles/intel-arc-pro-b70-multi-gpu-ai-inference-performance/)
- **Elect-rix impact:** ⭐ **Best VRAM/$ in the market.** Single B70 at $949 runs 8B models comfortably and fits SMB budget. 4-card config enables 27B–35B models but exceeds power budget. Quantization support (INT4/INT8) still immature in vLLM XPU backend — watch for updates. BF16 models (Gemma family) currently blocked.

### 🆕 Raspberry Pi AI HAT+ 2
- **Specs:** Hailo-10H NPU, 40 TOPS INT4 (26 TOPS INT4 vision), **8 GB on-board LPDDR4X**, PCIe (Pi 5 only), 3W max
- **Price:** **$130** (Pi 5 not included)
- **Availability:** Shipping since Jan 15, 2026
- **7B Performance:** ❌ **Not viable for 7B.** Jeff Geerling's review: Pi 5 CPU *outperforms* Hailo-10H on LLM inference because Pi CPU gets 10W vs Hailo's 3W cap. 8 GB RAM insufficient for 7B quantized models (which target 10–12 GB). Ships with 1B–1.5B models only (Llama 3.2 1B, Qwen2.5 1.5B, DeepSeek-R1 1.5B). Mixed mode (vision + LLM) didn't work at launch — segfaults. A user reported Llama 3.2:3b took 47 seconds to respond to "say hello."
- **Source:** [Raspberry Pi news](https://www.raspberrypi.com/news/introducing-the-raspberry-pi-ai-hat-plus-2-generative-ai-on-raspberry-pi-5/), [Jeff Geerling review](https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog/2026/raspberry-pi-ai-hat-2/)
- **Elect-rix impact:** ❌ **Not recommended for elect-rix LLM deployment.** Vision-only use case is strong (30fps YOLO, 10× faster than CPU), but LLM performance is a dead end at 7B+. For Pi-based LLM work, a 16 GB Pi 5 with CPU-only Ollama is better. This HAT is a dev kit for commercial Hailo-10H device design, not a consumer LLM solution.

### 🆕 NVIDIA DGX Spark
- **Specs:** 128 GB unified LPDDR5x, Grace+Blackwell, Linux only (DGX OS / Ubuntu 24.04)
- **Price:** **$4,699**
- **Availability:** Shipping
- **7B+ Performance:** 30B MoE model optimized for Spark; supports up to ~235B class models with multi-node clustering. Independent review (4 months later): mixed — strong for NVIDIA-optimized models, but price/performance for generic inference is questioned.
- **Source:** [NVIDIA product page](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/products/workstations/dgx-spark/), [Data Science Collective review](https://medium.com/data-science-collective/i-was-ready-to-return-my-dgx-spark-then-nvidias-january-update-changed-everything-e67699155a45)
- **Elect-rix impact:** Above SMB tier. Relevant as a reference platform for CUDA-optimized deployment, but $4,699 + Linux-only limits consumer/SMB appeal. AMD Halo undercuts by $700 with Windows support.

## 3. Market Signals

- **Edge AI hardware market:** $30.74B in 2026, projected to reach $68.73B by 2031 (17.46% CAGR per Mordor Intelligence). IDTechEx forecasts >$80B by 2036 at 18.5% CAGR. Grand View Research projects $118.7B by 2033 at 21.7% CAGR. All forecasts agree: strong, sustained growth driven by on-device LLM demand.

- **Desktop AI supercomputer category emerges:** NVIDIA DGX Spark ($4,699) and AMD Ryzen AI Halo ($3,999) create a new product category — unified-memory mini-PCs purpose-built for local 100B+ model inference. Neither fits consumer/SMB tier yet, but prices will erode. Watch for $2,000–$2,500 entrants by 2027.

- **Groq acquired by NVIDIA (GTC 2026):** Groq LPU integrated into NVIDIA data center rack strategy (Groq 3 LPU in early-access). **Consumer Groq hardware is dead** — no standalone product will emerge. Remove from consumer watch list.

- **Apple Neural Accelerators shift the GPU AI landscape:** M5's dedicated matrix hardware in GPU cores (Metal 4 TensorOps) is a paradigm shift. Apple is now a serious inference hardware vendor, not just "good enough." vLLM on Metal claims >400 tok/s batched throughput on M5 Max.

- **Intel Arc Pro B-Series enters MLPerf Inference v6.0:** Intel formally competitive in inference benchmarks with Arc Pro B-Series GPUs. Validates Battlemage as a production inference platform, not just a budget gaming card.

- **SBC LLM tooling reaching practical maturity:** RKLLama (Rockchip NPU) and hailo-ollama (Hailo NPU) now provide OpenAI-compatible APIs. The workflow is still complex (model conversion required), but the infrastructure exists. DeepSeek-R1 1.5B and Phi-3 Mini make sub-3B models viable on $100–$200 boards.

- **"Inference era" economics:** 80% of AI GPU spend is now inference (per Spheron). Cost-per-token math increasingly favors local deployment for steady-state workloads. Broadcom emerging as a custom inference chip winner for hyperscalers.

### Backend Priority (Updated)
1. **Metal (MLX / llama.cpp / vLLM)** — Apple Silicon. M5 Neural Accelerators + Metal 4 TensorOps + vLLM PagedAttention = best-in-class. Easiest deploy.
2. **CUDA (TensorRT Edge-LLM / llama.cpp)** — Jetson T4000/Orin + discrete NVIDIA. Widest model support. TensorRT Edge-LLM SDK is now open-source C++ — lower overhead than Python frameworks.
3. **Intel SYCL / oneAPI (vLLM XPU / llama.cpp SYCL)** — Arc Pro B70. 32GB at $949 is compelling. BF16 gap and quantization maturity are the risks.
4. **AMD ROCm** — Halo has full Day-0 support. RDNA4 consumer support still disappointing. Watch ROCm 7.x.
5. **Qualcomm QNN / ONNX Runtime** — Snapdragon X Elite NPU still underperforms CPU for LLMs. X2 Elite may change this. Deprioritize.
6. **Rockchip RKLLM** — Viable for 1B–3B on Orange Pi 5. 7B+ still below threshold. Useful for ultra-budget edge, not mainstream elect-rix deployment.
