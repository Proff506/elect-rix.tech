## 2. Updates to Previously Tracked Devices

- **Jetson Orin Nano Super Mode:** Free software update increased INT8 TOPS from ~40 to 67 without silicon change. This makes the $499 kit more attractive than earlier reviews suggested.
- **Apple Mac Studio M4 Ultra:** Now shipping with up to 512 GB unified memory. 128 GB config is viable for 70B+ models locally. Still priced above SMB tier at ~$3,999, but unmatched for inference-per-watt at that scale.
- **RTX 3060 12 GB (used market):** Prices have stabilized around $220–$260 on secondary markets. Still the best budget CUDA option for local inference under $300, though power draw (~170W) exceeds strict consumer tier.
- **RTX 5070 / Ti:** Launched early 2025 (MSRP $549 / $749). Improved efficiency over 40-series on FP4/FP8 inference via Blackwell features. Power draw 220–300W; not consumer-tier efficient, but strong SMB option for workstations.
- **AMD RDNA 4 (RX 9070 series):** Competitive rasterization, but ROCm consumer support remains a gap for easy llama.cpp/TensorRT-style local LLM deployment. Watch ROCm 6.x point releases.

## 4. Deployment Implications for elect-rix

**Summary assessment:**

- **Consumer Tier (sub-$500, <50W, 7B ≥10 tok/s)**
  - **Primary recommendation:** Apple M4 Mac Mini (16 GB) if budget stretches to ~$599 (or $499 edu). It is the efficiency king for local inference and mlx/llama.cpp compatibility is first-class.
  - **Alternative:** NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano Super ($499 dev kit). Best if you need a Linux edge node with CUDA ecosystem. Verified TensorRT-LLM path.
  - **Budget alt:** Raspberry Pi 5 (8 GB) + Hailo-8L HAT (~$140 total). Acceptable for 3B models; 7B is marginal and slower than 10 tok/s in most configs.
  - **Avoid:** Generic x86 SBCs without discrete acceleration for 7B—they will fall below the speed threshold on CPU alone.

- **SMB Tier (sub-$2000, reasonable power, 13B+ models)**
  - **Primary recommendation:** Build a low-power AMD Ryzen AI mini-tower (65W TDP) with 32–64 GB DDR5, or pair a modern iGPU system with an Intel Arc B580 ($249) for 12 GB VRAM.
  - **Alternative:** Used RTX 3060 12 GB (~$250) in a small-form-factor PC (e.g., Lenovo Tiny with PCIe riser or custom ITX). Proven llama.cpp/CUDA stack.
  - **High-end:** Mac Studio M4 Ultra is phenomenal for inference density, but exceeds $2,000 unless refurbed. Treat as "prosumer/enthusiast" tier.

- **Elect-rix Special Environment Compatibility**
  - **Backends in priority order:**
    1. **Metal (MLX / llama.cpp)** — Apple Silicon only; easiest to deploy and best efficiency.
    2. **CUDA (TensorRT-LLM / llama.cpp)** — NVIDIA Jetson and discrete NVIDIA GPUs; widest model support.
    3. **Qualcomm QNN / ONNX Runtime** — Snapdragon X Elite; required for NPU offload. Maturity improving but still requires custom model prep.
    4. **Intel OpenVINO / IPEX / SYCL** — Lunar Lake NPU and Arc GPUs viable but expect rough edges on bleeding-edge models.
    5. **AMD ROCm** — iGPU path is experimental for casual LLM inference; discrete AMD GPU (MI250 in Tinybox) is Linux-only and heavy.
  - **Avoid for now:** Rockchip RK3588 (Orange Pi) and RISC-V (Tenstorrent) for 7B+ LLMs unless you have dedicated porting resources.
