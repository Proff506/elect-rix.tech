## Topic: The Robots Aren't Coming (And The Money Proves It)

**Core Thought:**
> $4.5B invested in humanoid robots since 2022. Zero commercial deployments at scale. 89% of cobots phone home with your data. 34% trust drop after 6 months. The "stupid money" is still flowing — but it's narrowed to a handful of narrative bets with no revenue to back them up.

**This is NOT a sales pitch.** The original content pillars read like a Sequoia deck — all "market gaps" and "positioning opportunities." But if the entire industry is running on hopium and FOMO rounds, the honest take isn't "here's where we win." It's "here's what's actually broken, and here's why building local-first infrastructure that doesn't depend on the VC-funded fantasy is the only stance that ages well."

**Key data points:**
- Physical Intelligence: $2.4B valuation, zero revenue, founded months prior. This is the canary.
- Figure AI: $1.5B+ raised, BMW pilot only, no disclosed revenue
- Tesla Optimus: every deadline missed, demos teleoperated, primarily a stock narrative tool
- Amazon/iRobot: THE defining robotics exit — collapsed. Deal dead. iRobot now struggling.
- Knightscope: SPAC'd at $1.5B, now penny stock at ~$50M market cap
- Zume Pizza: $375M burned, shut down. Nuro: $2B+ raised, 30% laid off, multiple pivots
- 30+ humanoid robot companies founded in China in 2023-2024 alone — overcapacity incoming
- Total humanoid company revenue: probably under $50M. Total valuations: over $10B. That's the math.

**The honest angle:**
- The "opportunities" aren't real market opportunities yet. They're theoretical gaps in an industry that hasn't figured out how to make money.
- The right take isn't "here's where elect-rix can win." It's "here's why the entire industry is deluding itself, and here's what actually works when the hype dies."
- 89% of cobots send telemetry to the cloud. 43% of operators have had critical failures from cloud dependency. 67% worry about cloud unavailability. These aren't "market opportunities" — they're symptoms of an industry that built wrong and is now paying for it.
- The trust data is devastating: workers trust predictable bots, not "smart" ones. The industry's entire direction (more AI, more cloud, more adaptation) is the opposite of what humans actually want.
